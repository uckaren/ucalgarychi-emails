James Harrison: Linking Data for Better Information On Road Injury in Australia
James Harrison is an Australian injury epidemiologist and public health physician- he is the founding founding Director of Research Centre for Injury Studies at Flinders University and is the Director of the National Injury Surveillance Unit (NISU) in Australia. He is active in the areas of measurement and classification of injury, injury burden and determinants of injury in populations, and the descriptive epidemiology of injury for public health purposes. Join us at the Centre of Health Informatics for a chat about Health Information on serious non-fatal road injuries in Australia.
